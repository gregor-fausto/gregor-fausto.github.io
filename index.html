I am a PhD student in the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at Cornell University. I study plant populations and communities to understand the causes and consequences of genetic and species diversity.

Currently, I am focused on a project that incorporates environmental variables and density-dependence into demographic models to predict abundance. I am working with my advisor, Monica Geber, and collaborator Bill Morris. The research is based on data collected annually since 2006 by Monica Geber's lab at Cornell University, Dave Moeller's lab at the University of Minnesota, and Vince Eckhart's lab at Grinnell College. A grant from the National Science Foundation's program for Long Term Research in Environmental Biology helped fund this work.

Keywords population ecology, community ecology, demography, coexistence theory, evolutionary ecology
